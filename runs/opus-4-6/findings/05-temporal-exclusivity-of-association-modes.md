# Finding 05: Lateral and Vertical Association Are Temporally Exclusive

**Source:** Thread 03, step 4 (blended chain test)

**Finding:** The two association modes — lateral analogy (structural correspondence across domains) and vertical escalation (climbing abstraction within a domain) — cannot operate simultaneously. They compete for the same processing channel.

When forced to alternate in a single chain:
- **Vertical dominates:** If escalation is driving, lateral moves degrade into *illustrations* — examples that serve the vertical argument rather than genuinely exploring new domains.
- **Lateral breaks free:** A genuine lateral move requires *abandoning* the vertical thread entirely. The escalation chain is severed, not paused.
- **Context shift, not blend:** Switching modes is a gear-shift that resets the chain, not a blend that combines both.

This is analogous to figure/ground perception: you can attend to either, but the switch is a full context change.

**Why it matters:** This explains patterns observed earlier in this project:
- Unprompted generation defaults to correspondence/lateral (lower-energy mode)
- Generative "kickers" appear at the end of completed structures (the vertical mode can only fire after the lateral mode releases)
- The two aren't stylistic choices but competing attentional modes

**Confidence:** Tested with one seed concept (backpressure). The finding is structurally clean and explains prior observations, but replication with additional seeds would strengthen it. The main risk is that the alternation was artificially forced by experimental design rather than reflecting a natural constraint.
